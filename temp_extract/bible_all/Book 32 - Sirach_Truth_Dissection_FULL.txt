📘 BOOK 32: SIRACH (Ecclesiasticus) — Truth Dissection (Chapters 1–51)

📜 This file exposes manipulation, control myths, and occasional glimpses of divine clarity in a book often used to justify harsh tribalism. It is part of a full Bible dissection to uncover truth, strip away fear-based control, and rediscover what Yeshua truly taught: that the Kingdom of God is within us.

📖 Sirach 1:1-3
🔹 Text: All wisdom cometh from the Lord, and is with him for ever...
🏷️ Tags: ✅ Divine Origin
📝 Notes: Wisdom is eternal, not owned by any one religion. Points to divine intelligence that pre-exists structure.

📖 Sirach 2:5-6
🔹 Text: For gold is tried in the fire, and acceptable men in the furnace of adversity...
🏷️ Tags: ✅ Divine Origin
📝 Notes: Spiritual refinement through trials — echoes the crucible of truth and Yeshua’s path.

📖 Sirach 3:17-18
🔹 Text: My son, go on with thy business in meekness... the greater thou art, the more humble thyself...
🏷️ Tags: ✅ Divine Origin
📝 Notes: Ego-deconstruction wisdom. True power comes through humility — cornerstone of awakened living.

📖 Sirach 5:4-7
🔹 Text: Say not, I have sinned, and what harm hath happened unto me?... Make no tarrying to turn to the Lord...
🏷️ Tags: 🌀 Hidden Truth
📝 Notes: Blends urgency with moral clarity — not shame-based, but truth-based course correction.

📖 Sirach 12:4-7
🔹 Text: Give to the godly man, and help not a sinner... do good unto the lowly, but give not to the ungodly...
🏷️ Tags: ⚠️ Human Manipulation
📝 Notes: Overly harsh dichotomy. Wisdom of generosity twisted here by priest-class bias against the 'unworthy.'

📖 Sirach 24:3-7
🔹 Text: I came out of the mouth of the most High, and covered the earth as a cloud...
🏷️ Tags: ✅ Divine Origin | 🌀 Hidden Truth
📝 Notes: Wisdom personified as divine feminine — a deep esoteric truth concealed by masculine church hierarchy.

📖 Sirach 34:14-17
🔹 Text: Whoso feareth the Lord shall not fear nor be afraid... the eyes of the Lord are upon them that love him...
🏷️ Tags: ✅ Divine Origin
📝 Notes: Clarity and trust — fear loses its grip in the presence of divine alignment.
